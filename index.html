What are the main nutrients found in this ingredient?

Assuming a 100g serving, and assuming the eater has a 2000 calorie diet , Caviar contains 264 calories (13.2% DV), 25 grams of protein (50% DV), 18 grams of fat (28% DV), and 4 grams of carbohydrates (1% DV) when looking at macronutrients, and has significant amounts of certain micronutrients such as sodium (65% DV), vitamin B12 (333% DV), vitamin B2 (36% DV), vitamin B5 (35% DV), vitamin D (29% DV), and vitamin A (18% DV). It also contains large quantities of minerals, such as Selenium, Magnesium, and Iron (94%, 75%, and 66% DV respectively).

 History of Caviar

Caviar was invented in the 10th century, near the black sea, however it was only after the Mongol invasions of the 13th and 14th century that an industry developed.



How does Caviar fit into the Canada Food Guide?

Caviar fits into Canada’s Food Guide by supplying a lot of protein, as recommended by it [the Food Guide], as Caviar supplies 50% of your Daily Value of protein*.Caviar also fits into the “Use Food Labels” part of the Canada Food Guide, by considering 5% [DV] or less as a little, and 15% [DV] or more as a lot*. The micronutrients I have listed are more than 15%*, meaning it provides a lot of those nutrients. *This once again assumes a serving size of 100 grams, and the eater’s diet to be 2000 calories

What are some health benefits of Caviar?

There are many health benefits of Caviar, including fulfilling many neglected nutrients needs, prevent Atherosclerosis, improving joint & skeletal structures, and preventing blood clots, especially with red caviar

Are there any potential health concerns when eating Caviar?

Some nutritional concerns may include that it has a lot of protein and sodium, meaning you may eat too much sodium or protein in a day if you continue to eat high-sodium or high-protein foods throughout the day

How can Caviar contribute to healthy eating patterns?

Caviar can contribute to healthy eating patterns by fulfilling the daily value requirements of a lot of minor nutrients that most people don’t get enough of, like Vitamin D, or Magnesium.
